# 🔐 UPI Fraud Case Study

A first-year college student received an email that appeared to be from his bank. The email warned that his account would be suspended due to suspicious activity and asked him to verify his details immediately. It included a link that looked identical to the official bank website.

Without thinking much, the student clicked the link and entered his login credentials, including his account number, password, and OTP. Within a few minutes, he received multiple messages showing unauthorized transactions from his bank account. A large amount of money had been withdrawn.

After realizing something was wrong, he immediately contacted the bank and filed a complaint with the cybercrime department. However, only a small portion of the money could be recovered. The incident caused financial loss, stress, and a lack of trust in online transactions.

This type of cybercrime is known as phishing, where attackers trick users into sharing sensitive information through fake emails or websites. It is one of the most common and dangerous online scams.

This situation could have been avoided if the student had verified the sender’s email address and avoided clicking suspicious links. Banks never ask for confidential details like passwords or OTP through email or messages. Users should always check website URLs carefully and use secure practices such as enabling two-factor authentication.

This case highlights the importance of digital literacy and awareness. Being cautious and informed can help individuals protect themselves from cyber threats and ensure safe use of digital platforms.
